# Universal Ad Style Catalogue

12 proven ad styles applicable to any e-commerce brand. Each style defines visual approach, layout, psychology, and when to use. Brand-specific elements (colors, fonts, avatars, products) are pulled from the brand's Phase 1-4 documents at generation time.

**Notation:** `{{PRIMARY}}` = brand primary color, `{{SECONDARY}}` = brand secondary color, `{{FONT}}` = brand primary font. Replace with actual values from brand guidelines.

---

## 1. SCIENCE-FRIENDLY

**Psychology:** Cartoon-style anatomical illustrations make complex biology accessible. Anthropomorphized elements reduce anxiety. Playful execution balances credibility with approachability. Stories reduce counterarguing — viewers absorbed in visual narrative lower critical defenses.

**Visual Specifications:**
- Simplified anatomical forms with bold outlines (2-3px black strokes)
- Flat color fills (no gradients except product images)
- Cross-section or cutaway views showing hidden processes
- Cartoon anthropomorphization (subtle facial expressions, posture changes)
- Medical accuracy maintained despite simplified forms

**Color System:**
- Problem side: Coral/salmon (#FF9B8A) or warm warning tones
- Solution side: Mint green (#B4E8D4) or cool healing tones
- Neutral elements: White, cream, light gray backgrounds
- Product: Maintain brand colors from guidelines
- Text: Black or charcoal for maximum readability

**Layout Patterns:**
- Split-screen (50/50 or 40/60) with VS element at center
- Product at center of transformation (never in problem context)
- Multi-panel progression (2-4 panels for timeline/stages)
- Symmetrical balance between problem and solution sides

**Character Design:**
- Anthropomorphized product-relevant elements with emotions
- Sad state: Drooping posture, tired eyes, frowning
- Happy state: Upright posture, bright eyes, smiling
- Dignity maintained: Playful without childish or patronizing

**Humor Rules:**
- Humor enhances understanding, never distracts
- Contrast exaggeration between problem/solution states
- Friendly antagonist (fixable challenge, not personal failure)
- NO: childish elements (sparkles, stars, hearts), mocking, over-exaggeration

**Copy Guidelines:**
- Paired labels: Each problem element has corresponding solution label
- Scientific terms in accessible language
- Action orientation: "Removes Buildup" not "Contains Agents"
- Maximum 8-10 labels per image

**Best For:** Explaining invisible damage/processes, biological mechanisms, before/after at microscopic level, educational content needing broad accessibility, social media (highly shareable)

**Avoid When:** Immediate premium positioning needed, legal/medical compliance required, serious tone essential, real photography preferred

## 2. BA-EMOTION (Before-After Emotional)

**Psychology:** Dramatic transformation visualization triggers aspiration and loss aversion simultaneously. Desaturated problem state creates discomfort; vibrant solution state provides relief. The brain processes the contrast as a promise.

**Visual Specifications:**
- Perfect vertical split (50/50)
- Clear dividing line or VS badge
- Problem side: Desaturated, gray-scale or muted tones, cooler temperature
- Solution side: Full saturation, warm tones, vibrant brand colors
- Body language and environmental changes tell the story

**Layout:**
- Left = problem state, Right = solution state (universal reading direction)
- Product appears ONLY in solution side
- Minimal copy — let visuals drive emotion
- {{FONT}} Bold for maximum impact headlines

**Best For:** High emotional investment topics, conversion-focused content, retargeting audiences, social proof campaigns

**Avoid When:** Product requires rational explanation, target is analytical, topic doesn't have clear before/after

---

## 3. INFOGRAPHIC (Data-Driven Visual)

**Psychology:** Numbers create authority. Clean data visualization signals expertise and objectivity. Statistical proof reduces perceived risk. Visual hierarchy guides eye to most compelling data first.

**Visual Specifications:**
- Line-based icons (2px stroke, rounded caps)
- Connecting lines/arrows in {{PRIMARY}}
- Data visualization (charts, graphs, progress bars)
- Clean layout with clear visual hierarchy

**Layout Types:**
- Timeline (horizontal/vertical progression)
- Process flow (numbered steps with arrows)
- Statistics (large numbers with supporting context)
- Comparison grid (side-by-side feature breakdown)

**Color System:**
- {{PRIMARY}} for main elements, chart fills, connecting lines
- {{SECONDARY}} for highlights and accent data
- Charcoal/dark neutral for text
- White or light cream backgrounds

**Typography:**
- Numbers: {{FONT}} Bold, 72-96px for hero statistics
- Headlines: {{FONT}} Bold, 32-48px
- Labels: {{FONT}} Light/Regular, 18-24px
- Follow infographic design principles document if available

**Best For:** Educational content, mechanism explanations, social proof with data, comparison content, email sequences (breaks up text)

**Avoid When:** Emotional resonance is primary goal, topic lacks quantifiable data, quick scroll-stopping needed

## 4. NEWS (Authoritative Editorial)

**Psychology:** Editorial format borrows credibility from journalism. Third-person perspective reduces sales resistance. Readers process information as "news" not "advertising," lowering defensive filters.

**Visual Specifications:**
- Editorial headline (large, bold, newspaper-style)
- Subheadline or dateline
- Feature image (problem or research-focused)
- Body text columns (justified or left-aligned)
- Pull quotes or callout boxes

**Color Treatment:**
- Primarily black text on white background
- Minimal color (only in images or small accents)
- {{PRIMARY}} used sparingly for brand recognition
- Professional, credible aesthetic

**Content Style:**
- Third-person perspective (not "our product")
- Journalistic tone (reporting, not selling)
- Citations or research references
- Expert quotes or attributions

**Best For:** Advertorial funnels, cold traffic campaigns, trust-building for skeptical audiences, SEO content

**Avoid When:** Quick engagement needed, brand awareness (not authority) is goal, casual/fun tone required

---

## 5. TUTORIAL (Step-by-Step Educational)

**Psychology:** Numbered steps reduce cognitive load and barrier to action. Sequential format creates sense of simplicity. Hand-holding approach builds confidence in unfamiliar products.

**Visual Specifications:**
- Numbered steps (1, 2, 3, 4) in circles or badges
- Sequential flow (left-to-right or top-to-bottom)
- Visual demonstration of each step
- Brief description under each step
- Arrow connectors between steps

**Typography:**
- Step numbers: Large, bold, in circles (60-80px)
- Step titles: {{FONT}} Bold, 24-32px
- Descriptions: {{FONT}} Light, 16-20px

**Best For:** Product usage instructions, mechanism explanations, email onboarding, social media carousels

**Avoid When:** Product is self-explanatory, emotional connection is primary goal, single-image format required

---

## 6. RESEARCH (Peer-Reviewed Scientific)

**Psychology:** Academic formatting signals rigor and objectivity. Citation style creates implied endorsement from scientific community. Appeals to analytical decision-makers who need evidence before action.

**Visual Specifications:**
- Academic paper layout (abstract, findings, conclusion structure)
- Charts and graphs with citation-style formatting
- Conservative color palette with brand accent
- Dense but organized information hierarchy

**Color Treatment:**
- Predominantly neutral (white, light gray, charcoal)
- {{PRIMARY}} for data highlights and key findings only
- No bright colors or playful elements
- Minimalist, clinical aesthetic

**Best For:** Maximum credibility needs, analytical avatar targeting, ingredient/mechanism deep dives, competitive differentiation on evidence

**Avoid When:** Broad audience targeting, quick engagement needed, topic lacks scientific backing

---

## 7. COMPARISON (Side-by-Side Evaluation)

**Psychology:** Comparison format leverages anchoring bias — your product positioned against weaker alternatives looks stronger. Decision simplification through structured layout reduces analysis paralysis.

**Visual Specifications:**
- Side-by-side columns or grid layout
- Check/cross icons for feature comparison
- Color coding: {{PRIMARY}} for "has feature," red/gray for "missing"
- Clear winner signaling through visual weight

**Layout:**
- 2-3 columns maximum (yours + 1-2 alternatives)
- Feature rows with consistent spacing
- Your product column slightly larger or highlighted
- Summary row with clear verdict

**Best For:** Solution Aware avatars comparing options, competitive positioning, feature-rich products, rational decision support

**Avoid When:** No clear competitive advantage, comparison invites unfavorable scrutiny, emotional purchase decision

---

## 8. LIFESTYLE (Aspirational Context)

**Psychology:** Environmental context creates aspiration. Product shown in desired lifestyle triggers "future self" visualization. Association with positive environments transfers positive emotion to product.

**Visual Specifications:**
- Natural lighting, real-world settings
- Product integrated into aspirational environment
- Person interacting naturally with product (not posed)
- Warm, inviting color treatment

**Color Treatment:**
- Natural tones with brand colors as accent
- Warm temperature overall
- Product branding visible but not dominant
- Environmental colors set the mood

**Best For:** Brand awareness campaigns, aspirational positioning, social media organic content, lifestyle audiences

**Avoid When:** Immediate conversion needed, product requires explanation, target audience is analytical

---

## 9. TESTIMONIAL (Social Proof)

**Psychology:** Peer validation reduces perceived risk. Specific outcomes (not vague praise) create believable proof. Demographic matching to target avatar increases relatability.

**Visual Specifications:**
- Customer photo or avatar (match target demographic)
- Quote in large text with quotation marks
- Star rating or result metric
- Before/after elements optional
- Clean, trustworthy layout

**Layout:**
- Photo left/top, quote right/bottom
- Name + context (age, location, time using product)
- Specific outcome highlighted (not vague praise)
- Brand product subtly visible

**Best For:** Conversion campaigns, retargeting, email trust-building, landing page social proof sections

**Avoid When:** No authentic testimonials available (never fabricate), target doesn't value peer opinion, premium positioning (can feel mass-market)

---

## 10. LISTICLE (Numbered Content)

**Psychology:** Numbered format creates curiosity gap and completeness drive. Odd numbers (3, 5, 7) feel more authentic than even. Scannability increases engagement duration.

**Visual Specifications:**
- Large numbers as visual anchors
- Brief text per item (one sentence or phrase)
- Icon or small image per item optional
- Clean grid or vertical stack layout

**Typography:**
- Numbers: {{FONT}} Bold, oversized (60-80px)
- Item text: {{FONT}} Regular, 18-24px
- Title: {{FONT}} Bold with number prominent

**Best For:** Social media engagement, educational content, quick-consumption formats, Instagram carousels

**Avoid When:** Deep explanation needed, single focused message required, premium/luxury positioning

---

## 11. UNBOXING (Product Discovery)

**Psychology:** Unboxing triggers anticipation and discovery emotions. Reveals product quality through packaging. Creates purchase visualization — viewer imagines receiving the product.

**Visual Specifications:**
- Natural lighting, clean backgrounds
- Flat lay or slight overhead angle
- Multiple shots showing progression (sealed → opening → revealed)
- Premium materials visible (tissue paper, inserts, quality packaging)

**Best For:** Launch campaigns, subscription content, social media (Instagram, TikTok), email welcome series, gift positioning

**Avoid When:** Product packaging is basic, target doesn't value presentation, budget constraints on packaging quality

---

## 12. PREMIUM (Luxury Positioning)

**Psychology:** Minimalism signals exclusivity. Premium materials (marble, gold, dark surfaces) transfer luxury association. Negative space communicates confidence — the product doesn't need to shout.

**Visual Specifications:**
- Marble, gold, dark wood, or metallic surfaces
- Dramatic lighting with soft shadows
- Single product focus with generous negative space
- Rule of thirds composition
- Minimal copy (less is more)

**Color Treatment:**
- Predominantly neutral luxury tones (white, black, gold, marble)
- Brand colors pop against neutral palette
- High contrast, sophisticated restraint

**Best For:** Premium pricing justification, high-value customer targeting, gift campaigns, brand elevation

**Avoid When:** Mass market positioning, budget-conscious audience, educational content needed

## Style Selection Framework

### By Campaign Objective

| Objective | Primary Styles | Secondary Styles |
|-----------|---------------|-----------------|
| Awareness (cold traffic) | NEWS, LIFESTYLE, SCIENCE-FRIENDLY | LISTICLE, INFOGRAPHIC |
| Consideration | INFOGRAPHIC, COMPARISON, TUTORIAL | RESEARCH, SCIENCE-FRIENDLY |
| Conversion | BA-EMOTION, TESTIMONIAL, COMPARISON | PREMIUM, UNBOXING |
| Retargeting | TESTIMONIAL, BA-EMOTION, PREMIUM | COMPARISON, LIFESTYLE |
| Education | SCIENCE-FRIENDLY, TUTORIAL, INFOGRAPHIC | LISTICLE, RESEARCH |
| Brand building | LIFESTYLE, PREMIUM, UNBOXING | NEWS, TESTIMONIAL |

### By Avatar Awareness Stage

| Awareness Stage | Recommended Styles | Rationale |
|----------------|-------------------|-----------|
| Problem Aware | SCIENCE-FRIENDLY, NEWS, INFOGRAPHIC | Educate about cause, build authority |
| Solution Aware | COMPARISON, TUTORIAL, RESEARCH | Differentiate, demonstrate, prove |
| Product Aware | TESTIMONIAL, BA-EMOTION, PREMIUM | Validate, overcome objections |
| Most Aware | TESTIMONIAL, UNBOXING, LIFESTYLE | Reinforce, create urgency |

### By Platform

| Platform | Best Styles | Format Notes |
|----------|------------|--------------|
| Instagram Feed | SCIENCE-FRIENDLY, LIFESTYLE, PREMIUM, BA-EMOTION | 1:1, highly visual |
| Instagram Stories | TUTORIAL, TESTIMONIAL, UNBOXING | 9:16, quick consumption |
| Facebook Ads | BA-EMOTION, SCIENCE-FRIENDLY, NEWS, LISTICLE | 1.91:1 or 1:1, scroll-stopping |
| TikTok | UNBOXING, TUTORIAL, TESTIMONIAL | 9:16, authentic feeling |
| Email | INFOGRAPHIC, SCIENCE-FRIENDLY, TESTIMONIAL, COMPARISON | 2:1 header, breaks up text |
| Landing Page | NEWS, BA-EMOTION, SCIENCE-FRIENDLY, COMPARISON | 16:9 hero or 3:2 inline |

### Recommend 2-3 Styles

When the user doesn't specify a style, recommend based on this priority:

1. **Match to objective** (what are they trying to achieve?)
2. **Match to avatar** (which segment, what awareness stage?)
3. **Match to platform** (where will this appear?)
4. **Vary from recent** (if the brand has been using one style heavily, suggest alternatives)

Present recommendations with brief rationale: "For cold traffic targeting your analytical avatar on Facebook, I'd recommend SCIENCE-FRIENDLY (accessible education that stops the scroll) or NEWS (editorial credibility for skeptical audiences). INFOGRAPHIC could work as a third option if you have strong data points."
