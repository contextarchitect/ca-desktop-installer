# Root Cause Research Prompt (Fallback)

Use this ONLY when the founder cannot articulate why the problem exists and the root cause is not available in any existing documentation. This is a focused mini-prompt for Deep Research - not a full Phase-level brief.

Before using this fallback, try these probing questions first:
- "What does your product actually do differently from competitors?"
- "If a customer asked you WHY their problem exists, what would you tell them?"
- "What do other products in this space get wrong about the cause?"
- "Why do common solutions fail for your target audience?"

If the founder can answer any of these, you have enough to develop the narrative without research.

---

## Prompt Template

```
FOCUSED RESEARCH: Root Cause Analysis for [PROBLEM] in [AVATAR POPULATION]

Context:
I am developing marketing for a [PRODUCT TYPE] targeting [AVATAR DESCRIPTION].
The product addresses [PROBLEM] by [BRIEF PRODUCT DESCRIPTION].

I need to understand the actual root cause of [PROBLEM] at a scientific/biological/mechanical level
so I can explain it to customers in simple, accurate language.

Research Questions:

1. What is actually causing [PROBLEM] in [AVATAR POPULATION]?
   - Identify the primary biological/mechanical/systemic cause
   - Identify 2-3 key amplifying factors that make the primary cause worse
   - Explain why these causes are often invisible to the person experiencing them

2. Why do common solutions fail?
   These are the solutions our target audience has tried: [LIST FROM PHASE 2 COMPETITIVE CONTEXT]
   For each one, explain:
   - What it actually does (mechanism of action)
   - Why it provides partial/temporary relief at best
   - What part of the root cause it fails to address

3. What would a complete solution need to address?
   Based on the root cause analysis:
   - What are the 2-4 things that must be fixed simultaneously?
   - Why is addressing only one factor insufficient?
   - What is the logical sequence of treatment (what must be fixed first)?

Requirements:
- Cite credible sources (peer-reviewed research, medical literature, clinical data)
- Explain everything at both a scientific level AND a plain-language level
- Include at least one real-world analogy or metaphor that makes the root cause
  instantly understandable to a non-expert
- Keep total output under 2,000 words - this is focused research, not exhaustive review
```

---

## After Research Returns

Feed the research output into Step 1A of the skill. The research provides the raw scientific explanation - the skill then develops the 4th-grade simplification, refines the analogy, and writes the copywriting-ready section.
