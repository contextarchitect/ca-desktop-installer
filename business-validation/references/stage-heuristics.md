# Stage, Geography, and Category Heuristics

## Stage-Based Research Priorities

### Pre-Launch (Revenue = $0)

The core question: Does this problem exist, and will people pay to solve it?

**Research priorities (in order):**
1. Problem validation - Is the problem real and painful? Mine forums, Reddit, reviews for evidence.
2. Demand validation - Is there active search demand? Check Google Trends, keyword volumes, community sizes.
3. Willingness-to-pay - What do people currently spend on alternatives? What price sensitivity signals exist?
4. Competitive landscape - Who else is solving this? Are they succeeding or failing?
5. Basic market sizing - Is the addressable market large enough?

**Research instructions to include:**
- "Prioritize customer voice data. Find 20+ direct quotes showing the problem exists."
- "Search for failed predecessors. If similar products launched and died, investigate why."
- "Assess whether this is a 'stated preference' problem (people say they want it) vs 'revealed preference' (people actually pay for solutions)."

**Depth settings:**
- Competitor analysis: 5-7 competitors
- Customer voice: HIGH priority (20+ quotes minimum)
- Regulatory scan: Basic only (unless health/finance category)
- Financial modeling: Skip (no data to model)

---

### Early Revenue ($1 - $50k/month)

The core question: Can this grow, and what's blocking it?

**Research priorities (in order):**
1. Market validation - Verify market size, growth rate, segment dynamics with real data
2. Competitive moat assessment - How defensible is the current advantage? How fast can competitors copy?
3. Customer acquisition economics - Is the current channel sustainable? What does CAC look like at scale?
4. Customer voice mining - Authentic language for marketing, pain points for positioning
5. Basic compliance scan - Any regulatory risks that could block scaling?

**Research instructions to include:**
- "Validate current traction: is [revenue] realistic for this category and market?"
- "Assess competitive response capability: if this brand scales, how quickly could competitors react?"
- "Evaluate channel concentration risk: is the brand dependent on a single acquisition channel?"

**Depth settings:**
- Competitor analysis: 5-7 competitors
- Customer voice: HIGH priority (15-20 quotes)
- Regulatory: Category-appropriate depth
- Financial modeling: Basic unit economics assessment

---

### Scaling ($50k+/month)

The core question: What breaks when we push harder, and what's the ceiling?

**Research priorities (in order):**
1. Scalability constraints - Regulatory, manufacturing, competitive, channel limitations
2. Retention mechanics - Do customers come back? What drives repeat purchase?
3. Channel diversification - Beyond current primary channel, what else works?
4. Compliance deep-dive - At scale, regulatory exposure grows. What needs fixing?
5. Segment validation and expansion - Are current segments right? Are there missed segments?
6. Unit economics at scale - Do margins hold? Does CAC increase with scale?

**Research instructions to include:**
- "Deep-dive regulatory compliance: at [revenue] scale, enforcement risk increases significantly."
- "Analyze retention: is this a one-purchase business or does repeat purchase exist? Evidence?"
- "Evaluate channel diversification options beyond [primary channel]. What's the next best channel?"
- "Assess competitive response: at [revenue] scale, are incumbents likely to notice and respond?"

**Depth settings:**
- Competitor analysis: 7-10 competitors (include indirect and adjacent)
- Customer voice: MEDIUM priority (15-20 quotes, focus on segment validation)
- Regulatory: DEEP (full compliance assessment)
- Financial modeling: Detailed unit economics with sensitivity analysis

## Geography-Based Research Customization

### UK Primary

**Regulatory bodies to research:**
- ASA (Advertising Standards Authority) - All advertising claims
- MHRA (Medicines and Healthcare products Regulatory Agency) - Health product classification
- Trading Standards - Consumer protection, pricing claims
- CTPA (Cosmetic, Toiletry and Perfumery Association) - If cosmetics/skincare

**Health products specifically:**
- Melatonin is prescription-only (POM) since 1995 - critical for sleep products
- Health claims must comply with EU-retained Nutrition and Health Claims Regulation
- "Borderline products" guidance for supplements vs medicines classification
- Novel Foods regulation for new ingredients

**Customer voice sources (prioritize in this order):**
1. Trustpilot UK (most common UK review platform)
2. Reddit r/UKPersonalFinance, r/AskUK, category-specific UK subs
3. MumsNet / Gransnet (if female 35+ demographic)
4. UK Amazon reviews (amazon.co.uk, NOT .com)
5. Feefo, Reviews.io (UK-specific review platforms)
6. Category-specific forums

**Data and market reports:**
- Prioritize UK-specific market data over US or global
- Use GBP for all financial figures
- Reference Mintel, Euromonitor, or Statista for UK market sizing
- ONS (Office for National Statistics) for demographic data

**Advertising compliance:**
- Research recent ASA rulings in the same category
- Check if competitors have had ASA complaints upheld
- Note specific banned claim types for the category
- CAP Code requirements for the product type

---

### US Primary

**Regulatory bodies to research:**
- FTC (Federal Trade Commission) - Advertising claims, endorsements
- FDA (Food and Drug Administration) - Health products, supplements, food
- State Attorneys General - State-level consumer protection
- NAD (National Advertising Division) - Industry self-regulation

**Health products specifically:**
- Structure/function claims allowed without FDA approval, but disease claims are not
- FDA warning letter patterns for the category
- Supplement Facts panel requirements
- GMP (Good Manufacturing Practice) compliance

**Customer voice sources:**
1. Reddit (category-specific subs, r/supplements, etc.)
2. Amazon.com reviews
3. BBB (Better Business Bureau)
4. Trustpilot US
5. Category-specific forums

**Data and market reports:**
- US market data is typically most abundant
- USD for all figures
- NIH, PubMed for health-related evidence
- Census Bureau for demographic data

---

### EU Primary

**Regulatory bodies:**
- National advertising standards authorities (varies by country)
- European Commission - Product safety, labeling
- EFSA (European Food Safety Authority) - Health claims
- National medicines agencies - Product classification

**Health products specifically:**
- EFSA Health Claims Register - Only approved claims can be used
- Novel Foods Catalogue - New ingredients may need authorization
- Country-specific rules vary significantly (e.g., France vs Germany)
- GDPR compliance for customer data

---

### Multi-Market

When a brand operates across multiple markets:
- Apply primary market lens for main analysis
- Add a "Regulatory Conflicts" section noting differences
- Flag ingredients/claims legal in one market but restricted in another
- Note shipping/customs implications for cross-border D2C

### GCC Primary (UAE, KSA, Qatar, Kuwait, Oman, Bahrain)

**Activation rule:** Include this section ONLY when the detected geography includes GCC markets as a primary or significant secondary market. Many GCC-based founders sell exclusively to US/EU/UK — detect from the braindump whether the brand actually sells into GCC markets before applying these heuristics.

**Regulatory bodies to research:**
- SFDA (Saudi Food and Drug Authority) — supplements, cosmetics, food
- Emirates Authority for Standardization and Metrology (ESMA) — UAE product standards
- Dubai Municipality / Abu Dhabi QCC — local product registration
- Ministry of Health (varies by emirate/country) — health product classification

**Health products specifically:**
- Halal certification requirements for all ingestible products
- Import registration and product registration vary by country
- Arabic labeling mandatory for retail
- Some ingredients legal in US/EU may be restricted in GCC
- Traditional/herbal medicine has different regulatory pathway

**Beauty/personal care specifically:**
- Halal and alcohol-free positioning is a competitive advantage
- Climate-specific claims (humidity, heat, hard water) resonate strongly
- Premium/luxury positioning outperforms value positioning in UAE
- KSA market skews younger and more price-sensitive than UAE

**Customer voice sources (prioritize in this order):**
1. Instagram and TikTok (dominant discovery platforms in GCC)
2. Google Reviews (local trust signal)
3. Category-specific Facebook Groups (Arabic and English)
4. Reddit (smaller GCC presence but English-speaking expat community — r/dubai 300K+)
5. Local review platforms (Amazon.ae, Noon — growing)
6. WhatsApp group culture (hard to research directly but significant behavioral factor — note as influence channel)

**Data and market reports:**
- Use AED/SAR for financial figures (dual currency context)
- Euromonitor and Statista for GCC market sizing
- IMARC Group reports for Gulf-specific categories
- Dubai Chamber of Commerce reports for trade data

**Advertising compliance:**
- National Media Council (UAE) advertising standards
- No comparative advertising in many GCC markets
- Modesty and cultural sensitivity in imagery
- Ramadan and religious calendar affect campaign timing significantly
- Meta/Google ad policies apply but with regional content restrictions

**GCC-specific behavioral notes:**
- Free shipping expectations differ (UAE expects free over ~100-150 AED)
- Cash on delivery still significant in KSA (~30-40% of e-commerce)
- Subscription models less mature than US/UK — education may be needed
- Influencer marketing is disproportionately effective in GCC
- Expat vs national consumer segments have very different behaviors and price sensitivity
- Friday-Saturday weekend affects campaign timing vs Mon-Fri Western markets
- Premium brand preference in UAE — value positioning may underperform vs premium positioning

## Category-Based Research Customization

### Health & Wellness / Supplements

**Always include in research brief:**
- "Evaluate scientific evidence for each claimed ingredient/benefit"
- "Check ingredient legality in [geography] specifically"
- "Research manufacturing origin and quality certification requirements"
- "Analyze health claim compliance against [geography] advertising standards"
- "Look for category-specific enforcement actions and warnings"

**Red flags to probe:**
- Medical claims masquerading as wellness
- Prescription ingredients sold as supplements
- Undisclosed manufacturing origin
- Missing certifications (GMP, third-party testing)
- Claims contradicted by scientific consensus

### Beauty & Skincare

**Always include:**
- "Evaluate clinical evidence for hero ingredients"
- "Analyze ingredient trend lifecycle (emerging, peak, declining)"
- "Research influencer/creator landscape in this niche"
- "Check cosmetic safety regulations for [geography]"

### SaaS / Software

**Always include:**
- "Analyze churn rates for comparable products"
- "Evaluate switching costs from existing solutions"
- "Research integration ecosystem requirements"
- "Assess technical moat defensibility"

### General D2C / Physical Products

**Always include:**
- "Evaluate supply chain risks and manufacturing options"
- "Research fulfillment and returns economics"
- "Analyze seasonal demand patterns"
- "Check import/tariff implications for [geography]"
