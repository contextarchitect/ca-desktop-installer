# Voice Pillar Construction Methodology

## How Voice Pillars Work

Voice pillars are the 3-5 principles that govern how a brand sounds across all touchpoints. They're not adjectives (that's voice personality). They're behavioral rules with tension built in.

**Good pillar:** "Confident, Never Arrogant" (has tension: confidence without crossing into arrogance)
**Bad pillar:** "Professional" (no tension, no guidance on what "professional" means in practice)

**Good pillar:** "Scientific Without Being Sterile" (tells you to use science but keep it warm)
**Bad pillar:** "Science-Based" (no guidance on tone or delivery)

## Construction Process

### Step 1: Extract Raw Voice Traits

From brand guidelines, pull:
- Brand personality adjectives (e.g., confident, sophisticated, scientific)
- Brand archetype traits (e.g., Explorer = adventurous, Sage = knowledgeable)
- Tone descriptors (e.g., warm, direct, irreverent)
- Values-based voice cues (e.g., transparency = honest, premium = refined)

From avatar research, pull:
- Language that resonates with primary archetype (Section L)
- Emotional register that works (Section L voice/tone notes)
- Trust requirements (Section C relationship to authority)
- What alienates them (Section L language to avoid)

### Step 2: Cluster Into Tension Pairs

Group related traits and find the natural tension:

| Raw Traits | Tension | Pillar Name |
|-----------|---------|-------------|
| confident, bold, assertive | vs arrogant, aggressive, pushy | "Confident, Never Arrogant" |
| scientific, evidence-based, precise | vs cold, clinical, jargon-heavy | "Scientific Without Being Sterile" |
| premium, refined, sophisticated | vs elitist, exclusionary, pretentious | "Premium Without Pretension" |
| warm, empathetic, understanding | vs soft, weak, uncertain | "Empathetic Without Being Soft" |
| direct, clear, no-BS | vs blunt, rude, dismissive | "Direct Without Being Dismissive" |
| educational, informative, expert | vs lecturing, condescending, boring | "Educational, Never Lecturing" |
| playful, witty, human | vs juvenile, unprofessional, trying too hard | "Witty Without Trying Too Hard" |
| aspirational, inspiring, motivating | vs unrealistic, hype-y, pandering | "Aspirational Without Pandering" |

### Step 3: Write Pillar Definitions

Each pillar needs a 2-3 sentence definition that explains what it means IN PRACTICE for this brand. Not a dictionary definition. A behavioral instruction.

**Example:**
"Confident, Never Arrogant means we make bold, direct claims about our product without qualifying them to death. We don't say 'might help' or 'could potentially improve.' We say 'this works' and back it with evidence. But we never punch down at customers or competitors. Confidence shows in what you do, not who you belittle."

### Step 4: Write Sounds Like / Doesn't Sound Like

3 examples each. These should be CONCRETE phrases, not abstract descriptions.

**Sounds like:**
- "The science is clear. Transdermal delivery bypasses the gut entirely."
- "You've tried pills. They didn't work. Here's why."
- "48 hours. That's how long the active compounds stay in your system."

**Doesn't sound like:**
- "Our revolutionary formula may potentially help improve your sleep outcomes."
- "Studies suggest that transdermal delivery could be more effective."
- "We believe our product is better than the competition."

### Step 5: Test Against All Archetypes

For each pillar, ask: does this work for every archetype?

Example test:
- Pillar: "Scientific Without Being Sterile"
- Archetype 1 (Skeptic): YES, they need science to believe claims
- Archetype 2 (Newcomer): PARTIAL, too much science overwhelms them
- Archetype 3 (Pragmatist): YES, they want evidence without fluff

If a pillar fails for one archetype, add a "modulation note":
"For [Newcomer archetype], lead with the benefit first, then support with science. Don't open with the mechanism."

### Step 6: Assign Pillar Priorities

Not all pillars matter equally for all content types:

| Content Type | Primary Pillar | Secondary | Can Deprioritize |
|-------------|---------------|-----------|-----------------|
| Ad copy (cold) | Confident | Direct | Scientific (save for funnel) |
| Landing page | Educational | Scientific | Playful |
| Email nurture | Empathetic | Confident | Direct |
| Product page | Scientific | Premium | Empathetic |
| Customer support | Empathetic | Direct | Confident |
| Social media | Playful/Witty | Confident | Scientific |

## Common Mistakes

**Too many pillars:** 3-5 is the range. More than 5 creates conflicting guidance and the LLM can't balance them all.

**No tension:** "Friendly" is not a pillar. "Friendly Without Being Casual" is. The tension is what makes it actionable.

**Too abstract:** "We embody excellence" tells an LLM nothing. "We use specific numbers, named sources, and concrete examples rather than vague promises" tells it exactly what to do.

**Contradicting archetypes:** If one pillar says "playful" but the primary archetype is a burned-out skeptic who hates marketing games, the pillar needs a modulation note or replacement.

**Ignoring the category:** A health supplement brand and a streetwear brand can both be "confident," but the expression is completely different. Category context shapes how pillars manifest.
