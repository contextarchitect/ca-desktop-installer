# Listicle Framework: Three Variants

## Core Philosophy

A listicle is a purpose-built bridge between curiosity and purchase that works through scannable validation. Where advertorials lower defenses through narrative, listicles lower defenses through education — each numbered item builds a brick of belief until the reader arrives at the product as the obvious conclusion.

The critical insight: **listicles are not one-size-fits-all.** The right listicle variant depends entirely on what emotional/informational state the reader arrives in, which is determined by the ad that drove them there.

## Three Listicle Variants

### Variant A: Logic-Focused (Post-Emotional)

**Use when:** The ad or advertorial ad copy already charged the reader emotionally. They've felt the pain, connected with a story, and understand their problem. Now they need logical validation before buying.

**Tone:** Authoritative, clear, simple. Mechanism-focused.
**Each item answers:** "How does this product actually fix the root cause?"
**Content per item:** Mechanism, ingredient, scientific backing, how-it-works

**Example headlines:**
- "5 Ways [Product] Targets the Root Cause of [Problem]"
- "7 Reasons the Science Behind [Product] Actually Works"

**Image style:** Clean infographics, mechanism diagrams, ingredient visuals, process illustrations

### Variant B: Emotion-Focused (Post-Curiosity)

**Use when:** The ad created curiosity but no deep emotional investment. Day-in-the-life UGCs, short hook videos, curiosity images — the reader is interested but hasn't been warmed emotionally. The listicle needs to create both emotional connection AND product desire.

**Tone:** Aspirational, personal, relatable. Benefit-and-identity focused.
**Each item answers:** "Why will I love this product? How will it change my life?"
**Content per item:** Benefits, transformation, identity restoration, emotional payoff

**Example headlines:**
- "7 Reasons Women Are Finally Getting Their Confidence Back"
- "9 Things That Change When You Fix [Problem] at the Source"

**Image style:** Real people, iPhone-quality photos, relatable situations, before/after transformations. Authentic, not studio.

### Variant C: Product-Focused (Solution-Aware Audience)

**Use when:** The audience already understands the problem and just needs product validation. They're comparing options, looking for reasons to choose THIS product over alternatives.

**Tone:** Practical, reassuring, social-proof-heavy. Feature-and-convenience focused.
**Each item answers:** "Why is this product the best choice for me specifically?"
**Content per item:** Ease of use, convenience, results, community, value

**Example headlines:**
- "7 Reasons [Number]+ Women Choose [Product] Over Everything Else"
- "5 Things That Make [Product] Different From Anything on Amazon"

**Image style:** Product in use, real customers using it, iPhone selfies with product, lifestyle shots. People smiling (triggers dopamine). Normal people, not models.

## Page Architecture (All Variants)

```
1. Header (brand logo + shop link)
2. Headline + Subheadline + Opening paragraph (NO HERO IMAGE — text-first)
3. LIST ITEMS (side-by-side image + text, alternating)
   - ITEM 1: Image LEFT + Text RIGHT + CTA BUTTON after
   - ITEM 2: Text LEFT + Image RIGHT
   - ITEM 3: Image LEFT + Text RIGHT
   [Continue alternating...]
4. [MID-PAGE CTA CARD] — after around 40-60% of items
5. Remaining LIST ITEMS (continue alternating pattern)
6. [GUARANTEE SECTION] — after final item
7. [FINAL CTA CARD] — strong urgency, product image
8. [MOBILE STICKY CTA] — triggers at 40% scroll
```

**Key difference from advertorial:** No hero image. Listicles are text-first, jumping straight into educational content. The headline does the hook work.

## List Number Selection

**Always use ODD numbers (5, 7, 9, 11).** Odd numbers feel specific and authentic, not rounded or generic.

| Awareness Stage | Price Point | Recommended Length |
|----------------|------------|-------------------|
| Problem Aware | Low-ticket | 5-7 items |
| Problem Aware | Mid-ticket | 7-9 items |
| Solution Aware | Low-ticket | 7-9 items |
| Solution Aware | Mid-ticket | 9-11 items |
| Product Aware | Any | 5-7 items (shorter, objection-focused) |

## Strategic Item Sequencing

Items are NOT random. They follow a psychological arc that varies by variant:

### Variant A (Logic-Focused) Sequencing

| Position | Job | Content Focus |
|----------|-----|--------------|
| Items 1-2 | Establish the root cause connection | How the product targets the real problem |
| Items 3-4 | Build the mechanism case | Specific ingredients/features that deliver the fix |
| Items 5-6 | Compound credibility | Clinical backing, unique formulation, quality signals |
| Final item | Bridge to action | The "complete system" pitch — all pieces working together |

### Variant B (Emotion-Focused) Sequencing

| Position | Job | Content Focus |
|----------|-----|--------------|
| Items 1-2 | Mirror the pain | The biggest emotional struggles the product addresses |
| Items 3-4 | Show the transformation | What changes when the root cause is fixed |
| Items 5-6 | Build identity hope | "Getting yourself back" — identity restoration themes |
| Final item | Future pacing | The life that's waiting on the other side |

### Variant C (Product-Focused) Sequencing

| Position | Job | Content Focus |
|----------|-----|--------------|
| Items 1-2 | Biggest practical advantages | Ease of use, convenience, fits daily routine |
| Items 3-4 | Results and proof | Specific outcomes, customer results, timelines |
| Items 5-6 | Trust and community | Social proof, brand credibility, support |
| Final item | Value proposition | Why the price is justified, bundle logic |

## Image-Text Parity Rule

**MC Principle:** Text must NEVER overwhelm images. The listicle format works because it's scannable — balanced layouts where eyes alternate between image and text. Text-heavy layouts destroy the scannability advantage.

### Layout Specifications

**Desktop (>1024px):**
- Image column: 48% width
- Text column: 48% width
- Gap: 4%
- Vertical alignment: center

**Alternating pattern:**
- ODD items (1, 3, 5...): Image LEFT, Text RIGHT
- EVEN items (2, 4, 6...): Text LEFT, Image RIGHT

Creates natural zigzag pattern preventing monotony.

**Mobile (<768px):**
- Stack: Image on top, text below
- Both full width
- Gap: 24px between image and text

### Copy Constraints Per Item (ALL Variants)

```
ITEM BADGE: "REASON X" in brand accent color, small caps, 12px

ITEM HEADLINE (H2): Benefit-driven or mechanism-focused, 5-8 words

PARAGRAPH 1 (Core Point):
What's wrong, the key insight, or the product advantage. 2-3 sentences max.

PARAGRAPH 2 (Impact/Proof):
Why this matters, how it works, or social proof. 2-3 sentences max.

[OPTIONAL: 1 sentence product tie-in if needed]
```

**The parity rule in practice:**
- Maximum 2 paragraphs per item (3 only if absolutely necessary)
- Each paragraph: 2-3 sentences
- The text block should visually match the image height — if the text is visually taller than the image, cut words
- Remove ALL unnecessary words. Every word earns its place.

**Completion test per item:** Does this item make one clear point, supported by one proof element, in the space that visually matches the image? If the text overwhelms the image, it's too long.

## Opening Paragraph Structure

```
[Emotional hook — visceral problem description] (1-2 sentences)
[Specific symptoms — create recognition] (1-2 sentences)
[Frustration acknowledgment — empathy] (1 sentence)
[Transition sentence]
[Promise of explanation and solution] (1 sentence)
```

Hit search intent immediately. No meta-language ("In this article...").

**Variant-specific opening adjustments:**
- **Logic variant:** Open with the root cause insight, then promise mechanism-level understanding
- **Emotion variant:** Open with the avatar's deepest frustration, then promise transformation
- **Product variant:** Open with social proof or volume ("200,000+ women have already..."), then promise the reasons why

## Headline Framework for Listicles

### By Variant

**Variant A (Logic):**
- "5 Ways [Product] Targets the Root Cause of [Problem]"
- "7 Ingredients That Actually Fix [Root Cause] (Not Just Mask It)"

**Variant B (Emotion):**
- "7 Truths About [Problem] No One Talks About"
- "9 Things That Change When You Stop Fighting [Problem] Alone"

**Variant C (Product):**
- "7 Reasons Women with [Condition] Are Choosing [Product]"
- "5 Things About [Product] That Keep Customers Coming Back"

### Universal Headline Rules
- Odd number mandatory
- Specificity over vagueness ("7 Skincare Mistakes" > "Common Problems")
- No "In this article" openings
- Must align with the ad that drove traffic (see funnel-selection.md)

## CTA Placement Strategy

**Position 1: After Item #1**
- Button only (no card)
- CTA text per brand configuration
- Below item body copy, before item #2
- Brand primary color background, white text

**Position 2: Mid-Page CTA Card**
- After around 40-60% of items (after item #3 for a 5-item list)
- 2-column: product image left (40%), content right (60%)
- Light accent background
- Includes: headline, 3-4 benefit checkmarks, value proposition, CTA button, trust line
- Configured per brand

**Position 3: Guarantee Section**
- After final list item, before final CTA card
- Centered box, light neutral background
- Shield icon or guarantee badge
- Reassurance copy, no hard CTA

**Position 4: Final CTA Card**
- After guarantee section
- 2-column: product image left (40%), content right (60%)
- Strong contrast background (brand primary color)
- Urgency copy, system benefits, large CTA button, trust elements

**Position 5: Mobile Sticky CTA**
- Triggers at 40% scroll depth
- Fixed to bottom, full width
- CTA text per brand configuration
- 56px minimum height

## Image Requirements

- 1 image per list item (MANDATORY)
- 1-2 product showcase images for CTA cards
- NO hero image (text-first approach)

### Image Types by Variant

**Variant A (Logic):**
- Mechanism diagrams, ingredient infographics, process visuals
- Clean, educational, authoritative
- Split-screen comparisons (with/without)

**Variant B (Emotion):**
- Real people experiencing the transformation
- Before/after concepts (emotional state, not just physical)
- Relatable lifestyle moments
- iPhone-quality, authentic, not stock

**Variant C (Product):**
- Product in use by real people
- Customer selfies with product
- Lifestyle integration shots (product on bathroom counter, in morning routine)
- People smiling while using product (dopamine trigger)

### Image Specifications
- Aspect ratio: 1:1 (square) for list item images
- Format: 1200x800px PNG (or as specified by brand)
- Rounded corners: 8px
- Object-fit: contain
- Alt text on every image

### Image Naming Convention

```
listicle-[topic-slug]-reason-[number].png
listicle-[topic-slug]-solution-bundle.png
listicle-[topic-slug]-final-bundle.png
```

All lowercase, hyphens not underscores, descriptive but concise.

## Video Integration (If Brand Has Video Assets)

Video is NOT generated as part of this workflow. See `references/video-guidance.md` for full specifications.

**Maximum 1 video per listicle.** Listicles are scan-focused; video disrupts scanning rhythm.

| Position | Purpose | Max Length |
|----------|---------|------------|
| After mid-page CTA card | Product demo, mechanism explainer | 1-2 min |
| Replace one list item image | Deep-dive on specific point | 30-90 sec |
| Before final CTA | Testimonial or results compilation | 1-2 min |

## Scroll Depth Optimization

**Techniques specific to listicles:**
1. **Alternating layout creates visual zigzag** — eye naturally follows left-right-left pattern
2. **First image cut at fold** — partially visible below fold signals scrollable content
3. **Reason badges create progress markers** — "REASON 1", "REASON 2" signals progress
4. **No full-width dividers between items** — continuous flow, not segmented blocks
5. **CTA button after item #1** — engaged readers click early, others keep scanning
6. **Image-text parity** — balanced layouts feel premium; text walls cause abandonment

## Technical Requirements

- NO hero image (text-first approach)
- Page load: under 2.5 seconds
- Mobile responsive (stacks to single column)
- Semantic HTML (H1 → H2 per item)
- Line height: 1.7 for body text
- All images: alt text, optimized
- Sticky mobile CTA: 56px min height
- Tap targets: 44px minimum
- Tracking pixels: in `<head>`
- UTM passthrough script: in `<head>` (MANDATORY — see advertorial-framework.md for script)
- CTA buttons: centered, min 48px height
