# Category Reference: Beauty & Skincare / Haircare

## System Thinking Examples

### COMPLETION
- Shampoo + conditioner (cleanse + protect — one without the other is incomplete)
- Cleanser + moisturizer (remove + replenish cycle)
- Foundation + primer + setting spray (application system)
- Hair color + color-safe shampoo (treatment + maintenance)

### AMPLIFICATION
- Serum that amplifies moisturizer's active ingredients through better penetration
- Scalp treatment that enhances shampoo efficacy by improving follicle health
- Exfoliant that amplifies serum absorption by removing dead skin barrier
- Heat protectant that amplifies styling tool results while preventing damage

### PROTECTION
- Leave-in conditioner / hair oil (protects between wash cycles)
- SPF moisturizer / setting spray (protects skin between skincare routines)
- Overnight mask (protects and repairs during sleep)
- Protective styling products (shield from environmental damage — UV, pollution, hard water)

### RETENTION
- Cotton pads, applicator sponges, cleansing cloths (consumable tools)
- Refill pouches for shampoo/conditioner (eco + cost savings)
- Seasonal formulations (heavier moisturizer for winter, lighter for summer)
- Replacement brush heads, dermaplaning blades

## JTBD Moments Map

Map the customer journey across these beauty/haircare-specific touchpoints:
- **Morning Routine:** Cleanse, treat, protect, style — time-constrained, efficiency matters
- **Evening Routine:** Remove, cleanse, treat, repair — more relaxed, treatment-focused
- **Wash Day (haircare):** Full wash cycle — shampoo, condition, treat, dry, style
- **Between Washes / Between Routines:** Maintenance, touch-ups, refresh, protect
- **Special Occasions:** Events, photos, date night — elevated routine, premium products
- **Gym / Active:** Sweat-proof, quick-fix, post-workout refresh
- **Travel:** Minis, multitaskers, TSA-compliant, climate adjustment
- **Seasonal Transition:** Skin/hair needs change with weather, humidity, UV exposure
- **Problem Moments:** Breakouts, bad hair days, allergic reactions, product failures
- **Discovery & Experimentation:** Trying new ingredients, following trends, sampling

## Regulatory Considerations

**US Market:**
- FDA regulates cosmetics under FD&C Act — different from drug regulation
- "Cosmeceutical" is a marketing term with no legal definition
- SPF claims require FDA drug monograph compliance
- Anti-aging claims must be cosmetic (appearance) not drug (structure/function)
- Color additives require FDA certification
- MoCRA (2022) introduced new registration and adverse event reporting

**UK Market:**
- Cosmetic Products Enforcement Regulations
- CTPA (Cosmetic, Toiletry and Perfumery Association) guidance
- Product safety assessments required
- Ingredient labeling (INCI) requirements
- Claims must be truthful, not misleading — ASA monitors

**EU Market:**
- EU Cosmetics Regulation (EC 1223/2009)
- Safety assessment by qualified person required
- CPNP notification before placing on market
- Restricted/prohibited ingredient lists (Annexes II-VI)
- Claims Regulation (655/2013) — common criteria for claims

**GCC Market:**
- GSO (Gulf Standardization Organization) cosmetic standards
- Halal certification for cosmetics increasingly important
- Import registration varies by country
- Arabic labeling requirements
- Animal testing policies differ from EU

**Claim Language Boundaries:**
- Permitted: "visibly reduces," "helps improve appearance of," "smooths," "hydrates," "strengthens"
- Prohibited: "eliminates wrinkles," "cures acne," "regrows hair," "permanent results"
- High risk: before/after photos with implied medical results, "clinically proven" without backing study
- Haircare specific: avoid "regrowth" claims (medical), focus on "strength," "thickness appearance," "reduced breakage"

## Research Emphasis

- **Ingredient trend lifecycle:** Research where hero ingredients sit — emerging, peak hype, established, or declining. Avoid building around trend-peak ingredients.
- **Routine building is the moat:** The most successful beauty brands don't sell products, they sell routines. Research how competitors build multi-step routines and what makes customers adopt complete systems vs cherry-pick.
- **Texture and sensory matter:** Formulation texture, scent, and application experience are major purchase drivers in beauty. Note sensory preferences by market (GCC prefers richer textures; US trends lighter).
- **Refill and sustainability:** Eco-conscious packaging is increasingly a purchase driver. Research refill models, packaging-free options, and how competitors position sustainability.
- **Influencer and content ecosystem:** Beauty products live or die by content. For each candidate, assess how "content-friendly" it is — does it photograph well, does it have a visible transformation, is it demo-able?
