# Category Reference: Fitness & Sports

## System Thinking Examples

### COMPLETION
- Pre-workout + intra-workout + post-workout (training cycle coverage)
- Resistance bands + anchor attachment + door mount (equipment system)
- Running shoes + insoles + moisture-wicking socks (footwear system)
- Yoga mat + blocks + strap (practice equipment set)

### AMPLIFICATION
- Foam roller / massage gun that amplifies recovery from training (hero benefit lasts longer)
- Tracking device/app that amplifies training effectiveness through data
- Electrolyte mix that amplifies hydration beyond plain water during workouts
- Grip aids that amplify lifting capacity beyond unaided strength

### PROTECTION
- Joint support wraps / sleeves (protect from training-related injury)
- Recovery supplements / BCAA (protect muscle from catabolism between sessions)
- Equipment maintenance (mat cleaner, band conditioner — protect investment lifespan)
- Cooling / compression gear (protect against overexertion, speed recovery)

### RETENTION
- Consumable supplements (protein, creatine, electrolytes — monthly replenishment)
- Replacement grip tape, resistance band sets (wear-out cycle)
- Training program subscriptions (digital content extending physical product value)
- Seasonal gear updates (summer vs winter training clothing)

## JTBD Moments Map

Map the customer journey across these fitness-specific touchpoints:
- **Pre-Workout:** Preparation, nutrition, warm-up, gear setup, motivation
- **During Training:** Performance, hydration, tracking, equipment interaction
- **Post-Workout:** Recovery, nutrition, cool-down, cleanup, tracking results
- **Rest Days:** Active recovery, mobility work, meal prep, planning next session
- **Competition / Events:** Race day, competition prep, peak performance, specialized gear
- **Travel / Hotel Gym:** Portable equipment, travel-friendly nutrition, maintaining routine away from home
- **Injury / Rehab:** Modified training, rehabilitation aids, return-to-training progression
- **Gear Management:** Washing, storing, replacing, upgrading equipment
- **Community / Social:** Training partners, group classes, sharing progress, accountability
- **Seasonal Transitions:** Indoor vs outdoor training, weather-appropriate gear, seasonal goals

## Regulatory Considerations

**US Market:**
- Sports supplements: FDA DSHEA framework (same as health supplements)
- Banned substance risk: WADA prohibited list, NCAA banned substances, military prohibited list
- Third-party testing: Informed Sport, NSF Certified for Sport, BSCG — critical for competitive athletes
- FTC endorsement guidelines for athlete testimonials
- Product liability concerns for fitness equipment (injury risk)

**UK Market:**
- Same supplement framework as health/wellness (MHRA, ASA, EFSA claims)
- Informed Sport certification highly valued in UK fitness market
- Equipment safety: General Product Safety Regulations 2005
- Sports nutrition specific: pre-workout stimulant content scrutiny

**EU Market:**
- EFSA health claims for sports nutrition
- Novel Foods for newer ingredients (e.g., specific pre-workout compounds)
- Equipment: CE marking requirements
- Country-specific supplement regulations

**GCC Market:**
- Growing fitness market — cultural context matters (gender-segregated gym norms in some markets)
- Halal certification for supplements
- Heat and humidity affect product stability and format preferences
- Import regulations for fitness equipment

**Claim Language Boundaries:**
- Permitted: "supports muscle recovery," "helps maintain endurance," "contributes to normal protein synthesis"
- Prohibited: "builds muscle," "burns fat," "guaranteed results," steroid-adjacent claims
- High risk: before/after transformation photos, performance guarantees, implied PED effects
- Equipment: avoid injury prevention claims without substantiation

## Research Emphasis

- **Training cycle integration:** The best fitness product ecosystems map to the training cycle (pre/during/post/recovery). Research how competitors build around the training cycle and where gaps exist.
- **Athlete tier segmentation:** Casual gym-goer vs serious amateur vs competitive athlete have completely different needs, price sensitivity, and brand loyalty patterns. Each product candidate should clarify which tier it serves.
- **Banned substance risk is existential:** A single contamination event can destroy a sports supplement brand. Research how competitors manage supply chain testing and whether third-party certifications create meaningful competitive moat.
- **Community and content as product:** Fitness brands increasingly sell community access, training programs, and content alongside physical products. Research digital product extensions that complement physical hero products.
- **Seasonal demand patterns:** Fitness has strong seasonal demand (New Year, summer body, competition season). Research how competitors time launches and manage inventory around these cycles.
