# Category Reference: Food & Beverage

## System Thinking Examples

### COMPLETION
- Coffee beans + grinder (product needs the tool to deliver full experience)
- Protein powder + shaker bottle (format requires equipment)
- Specialty sauce + recipe cards (ingredient needs context for best use)
- Tea + infuser / brewing accessories

### AMPLIFICATION
- Coffee + creamer/flavor enhancer specifically designed for it
- Protein powder + flavor mix-ins that complement base formula
- Hot sauce + complementary spice blends that build on the same flavor profile
- Functional beverage + complementary snack bar with synergistic ingredients

### PROTECTION
- Airtight storage containers (protect freshness and potency)
- Insulated travel mugs (maintain optimal serving temperature)
- Proper storage instructions / humidity control packs
- UV-blocking packaging for light-sensitive ingredients

### RETENTION
- Single-serve pods / sachets (convenience-driven replenishment)
- Variety packs / seasonal flavors (prevent flavor fatigue)
- Subscription boxes with rotating selections
- Refill pouches at reduced price vs original packaging

## JTBD Moments Map

Map the customer journey across these food/beverage-specific touchpoints:
- **Morning Ritual:** First consumption of the day — convenience, speed, habit formation
- **Midday / Afternoon:** Energy, focus, snacking — portable formats matter
- **Evening / Wind-down:** Relaxation, indulgence, social — different flavor/function profile
- **Cooking / Meal Prep:** Ingredient use in recipes, portion planning, storage
- **On-the-Go:** Commute, travel, gym bag — single-serve, spill-proof, portable
- **Social / Entertaining:** Hosting, gifting, sharing — premium presentation, variety
- **Discovery / Variety Seeking:** Trying new flavors, seasonal editions, limited runs
- **Pantry Management:** Storage, shelf life, organization, reordering before running out
- **Dietary / Health Context:** Fitting into macro goals, allergen management, functional benefits

## Regulatory Considerations

**US Market:**
- FDA Food Safety Modernization Act (FSMA) compliance
- Nutrition Facts panel requirements (updated 2020)
- Allergen labeling (Big 9 allergens)
- "Natural" and "organic" claim regulations (USDA Organic for organic claims)
- Functional food claims — structure/function claims allowed, disease claims not
- State-specific regulations (California Prop 65 warnings)

**UK Market:**
- Food Standards Agency (FSA) regulations
- Natasha's Law (full ingredient labeling for prepacked for direct sale)
- Traffic light nutrition labeling (voluntary but expected)
- Health claims per retained EU regulation
- Novel Foods if using new ingredients

**EU Market:**
- EU Food Information Regulation (1169/2011)
- EFSA health claims approval required
- Organic certification (EU organic logo)
- Country-of-origin labeling requirements
- Allergen labeling (14 major allergens)

**GCC Market:**
- GSO food standards (GSO 2233 for labeling)
- Halal certification mandatory for most markets
- Shelf life requirements in hot climates
- Arabic labeling requirements
- SFDA (Saudi) and municipality-level food registration

**Claim Language Boundaries:**
- Permitted: "good source of," "high in protein," "no added sugar," "naturally flavored"
- Prohibited: "cures," "treats," "prevents disease," unsubstantiated superfood claims
- High risk: functional claims without EFSA/FDA backing, "detox," "cleanse," implied medical benefits

## Research Emphasis

- **Flavor fatigue and variety:** Single-flavor products lose subscribers. Research optimal flavor portfolio size and rotation strategies that competitors use to maintain retention.
- **Format innovation:** The same core product in a new format (powder → RTD → bar → gummy) can unlock entirely new occasions and customer segments. Research format preferences by segment and occasion.
- **Shelf stability and logistics:** Food products have shelf life, temperature sensitivity, and shipping weight constraints that affect margin. Every candidate should include logistics feasibility.
- **Sampling and trial strategy:** Food/beverage has uniquely high trial friction (taste is subjective). Research how competitors drive first purchase — sample sizes, trial packs, money-back guarantees, subscription trials.
- **Seasonal and limited editions:** Food/bev brands often use limited runs to drive urgency and test demand. Research seasonal flavor strategies and how competitors use scarcity.
