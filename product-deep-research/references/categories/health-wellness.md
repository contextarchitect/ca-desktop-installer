# Category Reference: Health & Wellness / Supplements

## System Thinking Examples

### COMPLETION
- Oral supplement + topical application (different delivery routes for same benefit)
- Daytime formula + nighttime formula (coverage across circadian cycle)
- Core supplement + cofactor (ingredient that improves absorption of hero ingredient)
- Capsule + liquid/powder (format options for different preferences)

### AMPLIFICATION
- Probiotic + prebiotic (synbiotic combination — one feeds the other)
- Supplement + diet guide/tracker (behavior amplifies ingredient efficacy)
- Absorption enhancer (black pepper extract, fat-soluble carrier, liposomal delivery)
- Stack builder (ingredients with synergistic mechanisms)

### PROTECTION
- Storage/travel case (protects potency from heat, light, moisture)
- Pill organizer with timer (prevents missed doses that break consistency)
- On-the-go format (travel sachets, single-serve — prevents skipped days)

### RETENTION
- 30/60/90-day supply tiers (natural replenishment cycle)
- Subscription refill pouches (less packaging, lower cost)
- Seasonal formulation variants (winter immune, summer energy)
- Consumable testing kits (blood panels, saliva tests to track progress)

## Regulatory Considerations

**US Market:**
- FDA regulates supplements under DSHEA — structure/function claims allowed, disease claims prohibited
- Check FDA warning letter history for the specific ingredient category
- FTC monitors advertising claims — substantiation requirements
- GMP compliance required for manufacturing
- Third-party testing certifications (NSF, USP, ConsumerLab) as competitive advantage
- "New Dietary Ingredient" (NDI) notification requirements for novel ingredients

**UK Market:**
- MHRA classification: supplement vs medicine distinction critical
- Melatonin is prescription-only (POM) — cannot sell as supplement
- Health claims must comply with retained EU Nutrition and Health Claims Regulation
- EFSA-approved claims list is the permitted ceiling
- Novel Foods regulation for new ingredients
- ASA advertising compliance

**EU Market:**
- EFSA Health Claims Register — only approved claims permitted on packaging and marketing
- Novel Foods Catalogue — new ingredients may need authorization
- Country-specific rules vary (France vs Germany vs Italy)
- GDPR for customer data in subscription models

**GCC Market:**
- SFDA (Saudi), Emirates Authority for Standardization and Metrology (UAE)
- Halal certification requirements for ingredients
- Import registration requirements vary by emirate/country
- Arabic labeling requirements
- Specific banned ingredient lists differ from US/EU

**Claim Language Boundaries:**
- Permitted: "supports," "maintains," "contributes to normal function," "helps with"
- Prohibited: "treats," "cures," "prevents," "heals," "reverses," "clinically proven to"
- High risk: any claim implying medical treatment or disease prevention
- Rate each product candidate's claim-drift risk: Low / Medium / High with rationale

## Research Emphasis

- **Ingredient evidence depth:** For every proposed product, research the scientific evidence base for hero ingredients. Cite studies, note sample sizes, distinguish human trials from in-vitro/animal studies.
- **Stack safety:** When proposing multiple supplements, investigate interaction risks and contraindication flags.
- **Manufacturing origin matters:** Note whether ingredients are typically sourced domestically or imported, and any quality concerns associated with sourcing regions.
- **Third-party testing as moat:** Evaluate whether third-party certifications (NSF, USP, ConsumerLab, Informed Sport) create meaningful competitive differentiation in this sub-category.
- **Subscription economics:** Supplements have natural replenishment cycles. Research optimal supply durations and subscription discount structures that competitors use.
